Squash is a vegetable that can refer to a flowering plant, a fruit, or a racket sport. 
Squash as a plant
Squash is a member of the gourd family, Cucurbitaceae, and is native to the Americas. 
Squash is a vigorous grower that is rich in fiber, potassium, and antioxidants. 
Squash is botanically a fruit, but is generally considered a vegetable when used in cooking. 
There are many varieties of squash, including summer squash, like zucchini, and winter squash, like butternut and acorn. 
Squash can be grown from seed, but squash blossoms require pollinators. 
Squash as a sport 
Squash is a racket sport played in a four-walled court.
The objective is to hit the ball so that your opponent can't return it.
The World Squash Federation (WSF) governs the sport, which will be included in the 2028 Summer Olympics in Los Angeles.
Other uses of squash
Squash can be used to make desserts or sweets, such as jam. 
Squash seeds and blossoms can also be cooked and eaten.
